    5|   According to Maxwell's equations, 
    6|         curl H = J 
    7|         div B = 0 
    8|         B = mu*H or H = B/mu
    9|  
   10|   where B is the manetic flux density
   11|         H is the magnetic field strength
   12|         J is the electric current density
   13|   and  mu is the magnetic permeability of the material.
   14|  
   15|   The magnetic vector potential A is related to B by 
   16|         B = curl A 
   17|   therefore 
   18|         curl( (1/mu)*curl A ) = J
   19|     if rmu = 1/mu
   20|     then 
   21|           curl( (rmu)*curl A ) = J    
   22|  
   23|   This equation is usually supplmented with the Coulomb Gauge condition 
   24|         div A = 0.
   25|  
   26|   In the axisymmetric case, the current is assumed to flow only in the
   27|   azimuthal direction, and only the azimuthal component of the vector
   28|   potential is present.  Henceforth, we will simply refer to this component as A.
   29|  
   30|   The Coulomb Gauge is identically satisfied, and the PDE to be solved in this
   31|   model takes the form 
   32|         curl((1/mu)*curl (A)) = J(x,y)     in the domain
   33|                            A  = g(x,y)     on the boundary.
   34|  
   35|   The magnetic induction B takes the simple form 
   36|         B = (-dz(A), 0, dr(A)+A/r)
   37|  
   38|   and the magnetic field is given by 
   39|         H = (-dz(A)/mu, 0, (dr(A)+A/r)/mu)
   40|  
   41|   Expanding the equation in cylindrical geometry results in the final equation, 
   42|         dz(dz(A)/mu) + dr((dr(A)+A/r)/mu) = -J
   43|  
   44|   The interpretation of the natural boundary condition becomes 
   45|         Natural(A) = n X H
   46|  
   47|   where n is the outward surface-normal unit vector.
   48|

   50|   continuity of (n X H) assumed by the Galerkin solver implies that the
   51|   tangential component of H is continuous, as required by the physics.

   55|   rotation lies along the X-axis. We bound the coil by a distant spherical

   18|         curl( (1/mu)*curl A ) = J
